TV Series

The Star Trek franchise has had a total of 13 television series, spanning over 47 seasons and 905 episodes. The series are listed below in chronological order of release date:

1. Star Trek: The Original Series (1966-1969)
2. Star Trek: The Animated Series (1973-1974)
3. Star Trek: The Next Generation (1987-1994)
4. Star Trek: Deep Space Nine (1993-1999)
5. Star Trek: Voyager (1995-2001)
6. Star Trek: Enterprise (2001-2005)
7. Star Trek: Discovery (2017-present)
8. Star Trek: Short Treks (2018-present)
9. Star Trek: Picard (2020-present)
10. Star Trek: Lower Decks (2020-present)
11. Star Trek: Prodigy (2021-present)
12. Star Trek: Strange New Worlds (2022-present)
13. Star Trek: Very Short Treks (2018-2019)

Movies

The movies can be group into 3:

The Original Series cast: This group consists of the first six movies, from Star Trek: The Motion Picture (1979) to Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country (1991). These movies feature the cast of the original TV series, such as William Shatner as James T. Kirk, Leonard Nimoy as Spock, DeForest Kelley as Leonard McCoy, James Doohan as Montgomery Scott, Nichelle Nichols as Nyota Uhura, George Takei as Hikaru Sulu, and Walter Koenig as Pavel Chekov. The movies explore various themes, such as the conflict with the Klingons, the search for Spock, the voyage to the past, and the final mission of the Enterprise crew.

The Next Generation cast: This group consists of the next four movies, from Star Trek Generations (1994) to Star Trek: Nemesis (2002). These movies feature the cast of the next TV series, such as Patrick Stewart as Jean-Luc Picard, Jonathan Frakes as William Riker, LeVar Burton as Geordi La Forge, Michael Dorn as Worf, Gates McFadden as Beverly Crusher, Marina Sirtis as Deanna Troi, Brent Spiner as Data, and occasionally Whoopi Goldberg as Guinan. The movies deal with topics such as the legacy of Kirk, the threat of the Borg, the rebellion of the Remans, and the sacrifice of Data.

The Kelvin Timeline cast: This group consists of the latest three movies, from Star Trek (2009) to Star Trek Beyond (2016). These movies feature new actors portraying younger versions of the original characters, such as Chris Pine as James T. Kirk, Zachary Quinto as Spock, Simon Pegg as Montgomery Scott, Karl Urban as Leonard McCoy, Zoe SaldaÃ±a as Nyota Uhura, John Cho as Hikaru Sulu, and Anton Yelchin as Pavel Chekov. The movies are set in an alternate timeline created by a time-traveling Romulan named Nero, who altered the course of history. The movies show the origin of the Enterprise crew, their encounters with Khan and Krall, and their adventures in the final frontier.
