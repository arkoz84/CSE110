TV Series

Star Trek is a popular science fiction franchise that consists of twelve television series and thirteen feature films. The franchise was created by Gene Roddenberry in 1966 and has been expanded by various writers, producers, and directors over the years. The main premise of Star Trek is to explore the galaxy and encounter various alien civilizations, while also dealing with moral, social, and political issues. The franchise is known for its optimistic vision of the future, its diverse and multicultural cast of characters, and its influence on popular culture and science1.

The most popular series are Star Trek: The Original Series (1966-1969) which was the first television series in the franchise, starring William Shatner as Captain James T. Kirk, Leonard Nimoy as Mister Spock, and DeForest Kelley as Dr. McCoy. Another one is Star Trek: The Next Generation (1987-1994) which was the second live-action television series in the franchise, set about a century after the original series.

Star Trek: The Original Series (1966-1969)

The series followed the adventures of the crew of the U.S.S. Enterprise, a starship of the United Federation of Planets, as they explored strange new worlds, sought out new life and new civilizations, and boldly went where no man has gone before. The series was groundbreaking for its time, featuring one of the first interracial kisses on television, a multiracial and multinational cast, and allegorical stories that tackled contemporary issues such as racism, war, and religion.

Star Trek: The Next Generation (1987-1994)

It starred Patrick Stewart as Captain Jean-Luc Picard, Brent Spiner as Lieutenant Commander Data, and many others. The series followed the crew of the U.S.S. Enterprise-D, a newer and larger starship, as they continued the mission of exploration and diplomacy in a more complex and diverse galaxy. The series was acclaimed for its sophisticated storytelling, character development, and philosophical themes, as well as its introduction of iconic villains such as the Borg and Q.

Star Trek Discovery

It stars Sonequa Martin-Green as Michael Burnham, a former Starfleet officer who joins the crew of the USS Discovery, a starship that can travel across the galaxy using an experimental spore drive. The series spans across different time periods, starting from the 23rd century, a decade before Star Trek: The Original Series, and then jumping to the 32nd century, where the Federation is in decline and new threats emerge. The series explores themes such as identity, loyalty, faith, and diversity, as well as the ethical and moral dilemmas of war and peace
